📱 What is SnackPet?

SnackPet is a free companion app that gives you full control over your smart cat feeder via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). Configure meal schedules, adjust portion sizes, sync the device clock, and review feeding history — all without internet or cloud accounts.

Your pet's data never leaves your phone. Everything is stored locally and communicated directly with the feeder over a secure BLE connection.

🚀 Getting Started

Make sure your feeder is powered on and placed within ~10 m of your phone before starting.

  1. Grant Bluetooth permission

    On first launch the App requests Bluetooth access. Tap Allow — this is required to find and communicate with your feeder.

  2. Scan for your feeder

    Tap "Find Device" on the home screen. The App scans for nearby BLE feeders and lists them. Select yours from the list.

  3. Wait for connection

    The App negotiates an MTU of 120 bytes and discovers the feeder's GATT services. This takes 2–5 seconds on first pair.

  4. Sync the clock

    The App automatically sends your phone's current date and time to the feeder so scheduled meals fire at the right moment.

  5. Set your meal schedule

    Go to the Schedule tab → tap ＋ → choose time and portion size → save. Repeat for each daily meal (up to 10).

  6. You're done!

    The schedule is written to the feeder. You can close the App — the feeder runs independently. Open the App any time to adjust meals or view history.

🔧 Device Communication Details

SnackPet communicates with your feeder using a proprietary GATT profile over BLE. The information below is intended for advanced users and developers.

Protocol Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) 4.0 or later
GATT service prefix 1cdb415c-a39b-462b-bd60-a19eb037180x
MTU size 120 bytes (negotiated at connection)
Schedule capacity Up to 10 daily meal entries
Schedule wire format byte[0] = entry count, then 3 bytes per entry: hour · minute · scoops
DATE_TIME_UTC format 10-byte BT SIG DateTime: year (LSB · MSB) · month · day · hour · minute · second · dayOfWeek · fractions256 · adjustReason
Connection steps requestMtu(120) → discoverServices() → characteristic interactions
Range ~10 m line-of-sight (typical BLE Class 2)

🛠️ Troubleshooting

Can't find the feeder?
Ensure Bluetooth is enabled, the feeder is powered on, and you are within ~10 m. On Android, also verify Location Services are on (required for BLE scanning on some OS versions).
Connection drops frequently?
Move the phone closer to the feeder. Thick walls and other 2.4 GHz devices (Wi-Fi routers, microwaves) can interfere with BLE signal.
Schedule not saving?
Stay connected during the save. If the feeder disconnects mid-write, the schedule may be incomplete — reconnect and try again.

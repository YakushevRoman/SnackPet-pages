🚀 Getting Started

  1. Power on your feederPlace the feeder within ~10 m of your phone.

  2. Grant Bluetooth permissionOn first launch tap Allow when prompted — Bluetooth access is required.

  3. Scan for your deviceTap "Find Device" on the Home screen. Select your feeder from the list.

  4. Wait for connectionThe App syncs the clock and reads the current schedule (takes 2–5 s on first pair).

  5. Set meal schedulesGo to the Schedule tab → tap ＋ → pick time and portion → save.

After the first connection the App remembers your feeder and reconnects automatically every time you open it.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

The App can't find my feeder during scan
  • Make sure the feeder is powered on and the indicator light is active.
  • Bring the phone within 2–3 m of the feeder for the initial scan.
  • Ensure Bluetooth is enabled in your phone's Settings.
  • On Android 11 and below, Location Services must also be enabled (required by the OS for BLE scanning — the App does not use your actual location).
  • Try force-closing the App and reopening it, then scan again.
The connection keeps dropping
  • Move the phone closer to the feeder. BLE range is ~10 m in open space but drops significantly through walls.
  • Avoid placing the feeder near Wi-Fi routers, microwaves, or other 2.4 GHz devices — they interfere with BLE.
  • Check that the feeder battery/power supply is not low.
  • Restart both the feeder and Bluetooth on your phone, then reconnect.
My meal schedule didn't save to the feeder

The schedule is written to the feeder over BLE during the save operation. If the connection drops mid-write, the schedule may be incomplete.

  • Reconnect to the feeder and open the Schedule tab to verify the current schedule.
  • Re-enter any missing meals and save again while staying connected.
  • Keep the phone within range until the save confirmation appears.
The feeder is dispensing at the wrong time

The feeder uses its internal clock to trigger meals. The App syncs your phone's date and time to the feeder at every connection.

  • Open the App and reconnect — this re-syncs the clock automatically.
  • Check that your phone's date, time, and time zone are correct in System Settings.
  • If the feeder was powered off for an extended period, its clock may have reset — reconnect to re-sync.
How many meals can I schedule per day?

The feeder supports up to 10 meal entries per day. Each entry has an independent time (hour + minute) and portion size (1–10 scoops, where 1 scoop ≈ 50 g).

Does the App require internet or a cloud account?

No. SnackPet is fully offline. It communicates directly with your feeder via Bluetooth and requires no internet connection, account registration, or cloud service.

How do I unpair / forget the feeder?

Go to the Settings screen inside the App and tap "Forget Device". The App will remove the saved device ID and return to the scan screen on next launch.

I reinstalled the App — do I need to set up everything again?

The feeding schedule is stored on the feeder hardware, so your meals are safe. After reinstalling you will need to scan and reconnect once — the App will then read the existing schedule from the feeder automatically.

What Bluetooth version does the feeder require?

The feeder uses Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) 4.0 or later. Any modern Android or iOS device supports this.

🔄 Full Reset Procedure

If the App and feeder are out of sync or behaving unexpectedly, follow these steps:

  1. Forget the device in-appSettings → Forget Device.

  2. Restart the feederPower it off for 10 seconds, then back on.

  3. Toggle BluetoothTurn Bluetooth off and on again on your phone.

  4. Rescan & reconnectTap Find Device, select your feeder, and let the App finish syncing.

  5. Re-enter your scheduleGo to Schedule → add your meals again.

If the feeder still behaves unexpectedly after a full reset, please contact us with your device model and OS version.
